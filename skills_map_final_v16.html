Transferable skills
Transferable skills
Skills from previous roles or contexts that apply to your target role, even if they weren't acquired in a tech setting.

Where to look: previous work, academic, or voluntary experience. Include anything that's genuinely relevant — project management, client communication, domain expertise, data work in non-tech roles.

Be generous here. Career switchers consistently underestimate how much weight transferable skills carry at junior level, where technical differentiation between candidates is often small.

Soft skills
Soft skills
The interpersonal and professional skills that determine how you work, not just what you can do.

Only add what you can evidence. "Good communicator" tells a recruiter nothing. "Presented findings from my capstone to a non-technical audience and rewrote the summary based on their feedback" tells them something real.

For each skill you add, write the specific example in the evidence field. You'll use these directly in interviews.
